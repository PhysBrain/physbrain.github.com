Oral Communication
What makes some speeches truly effective communications?
  • The messages are important.
  • The messages present visions that could affect the lives of the listeners.
  • The speakers establish themselves as an authority.
  • The speakers believe in their message.
  • The speakers get to the point rather than wasting the listeners' time.
  • The speakers use personal speaking skills, to get their points across.

Basic Blueprint of Communication
Aristotle broke communication down into three parts:
  • Message
  • Sender
  • Receiver
Sometimes refered to by the acronym MaSeR.
Effective Listening as a Communication Tool
Listening may only seem to be half of a conversation, but it is an essential part of effective communication.
  • Good communication begins with good listening
  • Non-verbal queues are also communication
  • A speaker should be listening to his audience
  • Communication is a two-way process
Engaging the receiver
  • Consider the needs of the listeners.
  • What does the listener want?
  • Speakers should seek feedback from their listeners.
  • What do I need to start doing?
  • What do I need to stop doing?
  • What should I continue doing?
Barriers to Effective Communication.
  • Lack of common ground
  • Lack of sincerity
  • Lack of authority
  • Lack of clarity
  • Poor presentation skills
  • Lack of receptiveness
  • Inconvenient or inappropriate environment
Ways to Ensure Good Communication.
Common Ground
  • The more we know about our listeners, the more likely we are able to find common experiences.
  • The greater the shared experience, the easier communication becomes.
Ways to Ensure Good Communication.
Sincerity
  • Care about the message
  • Care about the receiver
Ways to Ensure Good Communication.
Authority
  • Ideally the speaker should know what they are talking about
  • Otherwise, they should express a willingness to learn along with the listeners
  • Use communication skills to maintain authority while engaging the listeners in worthwhile learning activities
Ways to Ensure Good Communication.
Clarity
  • Speakers who care about their message and their audience tend to speak with calrity
  • Trying to hid part of the message or twist the truth often leads to fuzziness and confusion
Teaching of Skills
Effective communication is vital to the teaching of skills.
The Teaching Edge
Teaching a skill involves four very clear steps:
  • Explain how to do the skill.
  • Demonstrate how to do the skill.
  • Guide others to do the skills. Provide ongoing feedback.
  • Enable others to use the skills. Provide them with the time, materials and opportunity to use the skill successfully.
Summary
  • Communication is written, verbal, and noverbal.
  • Communication is essential to effective teams.
  • Communication is a tool of leadership.
  • Communication happens in the "common ground".
  • Communication should be clear and concise.
  • Sender and receiver consider one another.
  • Feedback is a gift.
