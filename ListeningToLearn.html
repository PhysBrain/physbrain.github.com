Discussion
Listening is an essential part of communication, yet we often take it for granted.
There are many courses that teach reading, writing, and speaking; yet seldom do courses teach how to listen.
Listening is a key leadership skill
Listening is a primary means for connecting with other people.
  • Sharing ideas creates a sense of familiarity and common experience.
  • From this grow trust, understanding and awareness of strengths and skills.
  • These are the building blocks of friendships and teams.
Listening is a key leadership skill
Listening provides the means to make decisions and solve problems.
  • It is the glue that holds a team together.
  • It is the doorway through which ideas pass.
  • It is the window in which solutions appear.

Two Parts of Effective Listening

Active Listening

Empathetic Listening

Active Listening
American Heritage dictionary
  • "Capable of functioning"
  • "Engaged in activity; contributing; participating"
Active Listening
Active listening reflects what a person is saying to confirm comprehension.
  • "What I understand you to be saying is ..."
  • Not making value judgements.
  • Just confirming that the message has been received correctly.
Empathetic Listening
American Heritage dictionary
  • "Understanding so intimate that the feelings, thoughts, and motives of one are readily comprehended by another."
Empathetic Listening
Empathetic listening is a sincere attempt on the part of a listener to understand in depth what a speaker is saying.
  • Pay attention to more than just the words you hear.
  • Also notice a speaker's body language, tone of voice, and emotional sense.
  • Consider all of these elements as part of the message the speaker is sending.
Empathetic Listening
Empathetic listening requires listeners to:
  • put themselves in the speaker's place,
  • imagine things from the speaker's point of view,
  • try to understand how the speaker feels.
Effective Listening is active and empathetic.
Effective Listening Exercise
  • Find a partner.
  • Choose a speaker and a Listener.
  • Speaker will talk for a few minutes.
  • Listener will try out different listening styles.
  • Speaker and listener will trade roles and repeat the exercise.
Effective Listening Exercise
Spend about 90 seconds listening with these behaviors
  • Pay close attention, but simply say. "I got it." Offer no further feedback.
  • Pay close attention, respond by rephrasing the message.
  • Rephrase the message and also share any deeper understanding gleaned from trying to understand the speaker's point of view.
Monitoring Our Listening Level
How is our listening ability affected by our own moods?
How do we respond when ...
  • we are hearing something we don't want to hear?
  • when a speaker is angry?
  • when we are tired or hungry?
Listening in Adverse Situations
Not every listening situation will be ideal.
Good listening skills are powerful tools for calming adversarial situations and finding solutions to problems.
Listening in Adverse Situations
Speakers respond to how others listen to them.
  • Acknowledge by don't immediately judge their complaints.
  • If there is no enabling by the listener, compliants will seem smaller and more manageable.
Listening in Adverse Situations
Take a negative and try flipping it around to a positive.
  • Listener can structure a more productive framework for finding solutions.
  • "I hear waht you don't want. Now tell me what you do want."
Listening in Adverse Situations
Casting a conversation in a positive light often requires more empathy and support.
Body language of listeners and speakers become more open.
Chances for resolution are greatly enhanced.
Listeners should always strive to create a positive present as opposed to a negative past.
Giving and Receiving Feedback
Giving and receiving feedback is a fundamental part of team development, leadership, and even freindship.
  • How does it feel to receive feedback?
  • How does it feel to offer feedback?
Giving and Receiving Feedback
Feedback is most helpful when both parties use effective listening.
Receiving feedback can sometimes be difficult.
By using effective listening skills, a feedback situation may be turned into a positive experience.
Tips on Giving Feedback
  • Consider your motives.
  • Determine if others are open to receiving feedback.
  • Deal only with behavior that can be changed.
  • Deal with specifics rather than generalities.
Tips on Giving Feedback
  • Describe the behavior rather than evaluate it.
  • Let others know how the behavior is impacting you.
  • Accept responsiblity for your own perceptions and emotions.
  • Make sure others have understood your message.
You can give caring feedback without a good technique, but the slickest technique in the world will not hide a lack of caring.
Tips on Receiving Feedback
  • Seek out feedback.
  • Listen carefully.
  • Listen actively.
  • Listen empathetically.
  • Notice how you are feeling.
Consider feedback to be a gift.
It truly is.
Summary
  • Effective listening is a skill that each of us can learn and constantly improve upon.
  • Listening plays a vital role in forming relationships, developing teams, and finding solutions.
  • The best listening is both active and empathetic.
  • Listening can be a tool for turning a negative situation into a positive one.
  • Listening well is an important part of both giving and receiving feedback.
  • Feedback is a gift.
